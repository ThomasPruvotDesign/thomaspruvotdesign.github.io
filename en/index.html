ABOUT

Thomas Pruvot creates pieces of sculpted light in wood, traditional gesso and natural minerals, made as unique pieces in his workshop in Chennevières-sur-Marne. Trained in decorative painting techniques and ancient plasters, he has developed a personal approach to gesso by combining it with translucent stones – agate, amethyst, quartz, calcite – to reveal a soft and contained light rather than frontal. His objects – lamps, mirrors, consoles – are designed as silent presences in space. They find their place in exceptional interiors, contemplative spaces, hotels or galleries where one seeks a mineral, discreet and lasting light.

His creations are featured at Salon Baba (Paris 8th) and at the L'Œil de Mercure bookstore (Paris 5th), and have been added to Studio KO's materials library.

"I believe that beauty is not a substance in itself, but merely a pattern of shadows, a play of chiaroscuro produced by the juxtaposition of different substances." Jun'ichirô Tanizaki, In Praise of Shadows

An interview with Thomas Pruvot about his journey and his approach to art craftsmanship is available in the program "Le libre journal des artisans" (Radio Courtoisie).

LIGHTNING

These luminaires are designed as contemplative pieces, where light gradually reveals the qualities of minerals, gesso and sculpted wood. Minerals (Brazilian agate, rose quartz, Indian amethyst, salt agate), gesso worked according to ancestral techniques and solid sipo wood finely gouged by hand are put at the service of calm atmospheres, conducive to rest, reading or meditation. Each piece aims less to illuminate strongly than to install a soft luminous presence, favorable to serenity and concentration.

Eclat

Indian salt agate, silvered gesso, gouged sipo wood
42 × 33 × 16 cm

The salt agate allows a soft, clear light to filter through without dazzling. The silver gesso responds to the hand-carved sipo wood and emphasizes the mineral presence of the piece. "Eclat" is designed for bright living rooms, offices or reception spaces where light must remain both precise and soothing.

Céleste

Indian amethyst, "Peau de gesso", hand-gouged sipo wood
42 × 33 × 16 cm

A creation conducive to reading, study and moments of withdrawal. The amethyst diffuses a deep and calm light, while the frame in Gesso Skin and finely worked sipo wood brings a tactile and visual presence that supports concentration and contemplation.

Intime

Indian rose quartz, cracked pink gesso with pearlescent nuances, gouged sipo wood
42 × 33 × 16 cm

"Intime" works with the softness of rose quartz: a delicate light with soothing pink tones. The crackled gesso with pearlescent reflections reinforces this impression of calm and proximity. The piece naturally finds its place in a bedroom or living room where one seeks discreet and warm light.

Chartres

Brazilian agate mosaic, "Peau de gesso", gouged sipo wood
42 × 33 × 16 cm

Inspired by the cathedral of Chartres and its medieval stained glass windows, this piece works with a mosaic of natural Brazilian agates in warm tones. The light, filtered through the stone, recalls the atmosphere of stained glass while remaining contained and enveloping. Chartres is designed for spaces where one seeks warm light, conducive to recollection.

Emanation

Indian amethyst, gouged wenge wood frame, "Peau de gesso" inlaid with copper
52 × 24 × 16 cm

Luminous stele made from a hand-carved amethyst block, pierced with a central light source. The light diffuses indirectly, tinted with violet and orange reflections, creating a warm and intimate penumbra. Designed for contemplative spaces – places of worship, exceptional hotels, architectural residences – "Emanation" brings a vertical and silent presence. Its atmosphere evokes the world of Serge Lutens perfumes, between mystery and restraint.

Presence

"Chartres" and "Emanation" have been integrated into varied contexts, from intimate living rooms to sacred spaces, where their mineral light discreetly transforms the atmosphere of the place.

GESSO FURNITURE

COLLECTION ORGANIQUE

The Organic collection was born from observing natural lines: soft curves, rounded forms that bring a vegetal presence into the space. In the workshop, patterns are created from mica pieces; depending on the finishes, gesso can evoke leather, animal skin or a softened mineral material. Each piece emphasizes line and texture rather than ornamentation.

Console Mica

Gesso marquetry console developed from mica pieces whose texture evokes leather or animal skin. The beech structure is entirely covered with pieces laid in accumulation, and the glass top placed above protects and highlights the surface. The base is set with aluminum, which reinforces the frontal and mineral character of the piece.

Each of these pieces extends ancestral know-how, reinterpreted with contemporary sensitivity, and invites intimate contemplation of everyday life.

COLLECTION COSMATI

In the "Cosmati" collection appears a deep geometric dimension. The gesso pieces are cut according to the phi ratio, giving them a strong symbolic charge, inspired by medieval Italian mosaics. The term "Cosmati" refers to the Roman artisans of the Middle Ages whose pavements still adorn many churches in Italy today, and recalls the timeless beauty of sacred geometry and so-called divine proportions.

PEAU DE GESSO

The "Peau de Gesso" collection was born as the logical continuation and synthesis of the first two collections. It combines geometric precision with an organic texture, evoking phyllotaxis, this harmonious organization of forms in nature. The surface, which recalls a snake or pangolin skin, offers a tactile and visual material of great richness, capturing and reflecting light in an almost hypnotic way.
